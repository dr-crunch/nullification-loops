### I. Legal Basis and Governance

The rotation of the Presidency is governed by **Article 16(9)** of the Treaty on European Union (TEU) and **Article 236** of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union (TFEU). The current specific order was established by **Council Decision (EU) 2016/1316**, which revised the schedule through December 31, 2030, following the United Kingdom's withdrawal from the European Union.

### III. The "Trio" System

To ensure continuity across the six-month terms, Member States work in groups of three, known as **"Trios."** These trios prepare a shared 18-month program of long-term goals and a common agenda.

- **Current Trio (2025‚Äì2026):** Poland, Denmark, and Cyprus.
    
- **Next Trio (2026‚Äì2027):** Ireland, Lithuania, and Greece.
    

### IV. Functional Responsibilities

The Member State holding the Presidency is responsible for:

1. **Chairing Meetings:** Leading sessions of the Council (except the Foreign Affairs Council, which is chaired by the High Representative).
    
2. **Agenda Setting:** Determining the priority of legislative files and brokering compromises between Member States.
    
3. **Representation:** Representing the Council in negotiations with other EU institutions, such as the European Parliament and the Commission (trilogues).
